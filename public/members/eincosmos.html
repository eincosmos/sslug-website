I didn't learn computers. I broke them repeatedly until they made sense.

My relationship with computers started young. No outdoor hobbies, just a screen, a dodgy internet connection, and an unshakeable curiosity about how things worked under the hood. Missing DLL files, softbricked phones, failed OS flashes each one was an accidental lesson in how systems actually behave when you push them wrong. In-app purchases only through lucky patcher. Rooting every Android device I owned felt less like a hobby and more like a necessity. Discovering termux was the best thing LOL.

That chaos eventually turned into something useful. I started understanding why things broke bootloaders, partitions, system libraries, kernel modules. One thing led to another and now I run Arch Linux, build embedded firmware on ESP32, and spend way too much time at the intersection of hardware and low-level software. Lately, I've been finding fun in learning assembly language and exploring low-level computing.

Currently studying CSE with an IoT specialization at SNU Chennai. The curiosity never left;it just got a compiler.
